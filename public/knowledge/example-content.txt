## Trading Basics

### What is a Candlestick?
A candlestick is a type of price chart used in technical analysis that displays the high, low, open, and close prices of a security for a specific period.

Components:
- Body: The area between open and close
- Upper Shadow (Wick): High of the period
- Lower Shadow (Wick): Low of the period
- Green/White: Close > Open (Bullish)
- Red/Black: Close < Open (Bearish)

## Common Candlestick Patterns

### 1. Hammer
- Appears at bottom of downtrend
- Small body at top, long lower shadow
- Signals potential bullish reversal
- Lower shadow should be 2-3x body length

### 2. Shooting Star
- Appears at top of uptrend
- Small body at bottom, long upper shadow
- Signals potential bearish reversal
- Upper shadow should be 2-3x body length

### 3. Doji
- Open and close are equal/very close
- Represents indecision in the market
- Can signal reversal when combined with other patterns
- Types: Standard, Long-legged, Gravestone, Dragonfly

### 4. Engulfing Patterns
Bullish Engulfing:
- Occurs after downtrend
- Green candle completely engulfs previous red candle
- Strong reversal signal

Bearish Engulfing:
- Occurs after uptrend
- Red candle completely engulfs previous green candle
- Strong reversal signal

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- Never risk more than 1-2% of capital per trade
- Calculate position size based on stop loss distance
- Use proper leverage (or none at all for beginners)

### Stop Loss Rules
- Always set stop loss before entering trade
- Place beyond technical levels (support/resistance)
- Don't move stop loss against your position
- Accept the loss if hit

### Psychology
- Emotions are the biggest enemy
- Stick to your trading plan
- Don't revenge trade after losses
- Take breaks after big wins or losses
- Keep a trading journal

## Trading Strategies

### Support and Resistance Trading
- Identify key levels where price bounces
- Buy near support, sell near resistance
- Wait for confirmation before entry
- Use multiple timeframes for confirmation

### Trend Following
- Trade in direction of major trend
- Use moving averages to identify trend
- Enter on pullbacks in trending markets
- Don't fight the trend

### Breakout Trading
- Wait for price to break key levels
- Confirm with volume
- Enter after successful retest
- Set stop below breakout level

## Common Mistakes

1. **Overtrading**: Taking too many trades
2. **Lack of Plan**: Trading without strategy
3. **Ignoring Risk Management**: Not using stop losses
4. **Emotional Trading**: Letting fear/greed control decisions
5. **Not Learning**: Repeating same mistakes

## Key Principles

✅ The trend is your friend
✅ Cut losses short, let profits run
✅ Plan your trade, trade your plan
✅ Risk management > Everything else
✅ Consistency beats perfection
